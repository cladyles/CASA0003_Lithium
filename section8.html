Annual Global EV Sales (2010-2023)

Top EV Sales Countries

1. J-shaped Growth: EV sales rose from ~10⁴ units in 2010–2014 to >10⁷ units by 2023.

2. China-Led Market: China supplies >50 % of global EVs since 2018, with the US and EU as key secondary markets.

3. Policy-Chain Synergy: Coordinated subsidies, infrastructure and battery capacity support 20–30 % annual growth.

4. Cost Parity: Battery pack costs fell >80 % since 2010, bringing many BEVs to total-cost-of-ownership parity with ICE cars by 2023.

5. Charging Roll-out: Public chargers grew from <0.1 M in 2015 to >3 M in 2023; regions with dense fast-charger networks saw adoption rates ~1.5× higher.

6. Model Proliferation: Global EV variants jumped from <10 in 2010 to >200 in 2023, with luxury EVs (>$70 k) making up ~20 % of sales.

A blooming of EV sales in China

China’s electric-vehicle surge isn’t the result of one silver bullet but of a tight weave of policy, scale, supply-chain muscle and relentless competition. The strands reinforce each other, so the market keeps accelerating even after some purchase subsidies have ended.

A fully integrated, low-cost battery & parts supply chain has been the key driver of this boom. There is also a home market vast enough to fund rapid iteration. Furthermore, more and more tech firms are joining the industry, and this hyper-competitive innovation loop keeps boosting EV development.
