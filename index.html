Bright Future

Lithium

As the world accelerates its transition to green energy, lithium has become a key mineral driving this transformation. Lithium is at the core of the development of rechargeable batteries, spurring innovation in the fields of energy storage and electric vehicles, making it an important component in the global move towards a more sustainable and decarbonized future.

Read More

The first commercial lithium-ion battery was introduced in 1991, with only a short period between laboratory research and industrial production. But it completely revolutionized our way of life in a very short time and was immediately regarded as a truly revolutionary technology and key energy transition mineral. Today, lithium batteries power most tools, electronic devices, smartphones, Laptops, and watches, as well as bicycles, scooters, cars, and machinery across various industries. In other words, most of the items we use daily rely on the energy provided by lithium-ion batteries to operate. Overall, whether from a research or commercial market development perspective, the lithium battery chain is exciting. According to a forecast, the Li-ion batteries chain, from mining to recycling, could see an annual growth rate exceeding 30% from 2022 to 2030, with its value surpassing $400 billion and the market size reaching 4.7 TWh.

Li-ion Batteries & EV Industry

Backbone of the modern EV revolution

Today’s lithium‐ion cells consist of two electrodes:
a cathode and an anode.

The cathode is the most complex component, with its mix of lithium, cobalt, manganese, iron, and other minerals determining overall performance.

In a single EV battery cell, the cathode (positive) and anode (negative) are separated by a porous membrane and immersed in an electrolyte through which lithium ions migrate. During discharge, lithium ions flow from cathode to anode to produce electrical power; charging reverses this reaction, driving ions back to the cathode for storage.

Battery Pack: Stores and manages energy flow for propulsion and accessories.
Electric Motor: Converts stored electrical energy into mechanical drive force.
Charging Port Provides the interface for external power to recharge the battery pack.
Anode: Releases Li⁺ and electrons during discharge; re-absorbs Li⁺ during charging.
Electrolyte: Conducts Li⁺ between electrodes while preventing electron flow; ensures stable ion transport.
